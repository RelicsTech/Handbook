Section I: Responding To Tickets

General Guidelines

When responding to a ticket, keep all communication strictly inside of the ticket. Never ask for any personal information from the user.

Important Notice:

If the user's problem involves their physical computer/laptop and you need to guide them to open it and fix the issue, you must copy and paste the following message to them. They must agree before you continue:

"By opening one of these tickets you agree that although we will never give you false information, we do not take any responsibility if your computer breaks or is damaged in the process of seeking support. Say I Agree/Disagree To Continue"

If they agree, you may proceed with the support. If they disagree, you cannot continue with physical troubleshooting.

Claiming Tickets

To claim a ticket, click the 'Claim' button at the bottom of the welcome text from Ticktey.

Section II: Tech Support/Troubleshooting Tickets

Handling Tech Support Tickets

In this type of ticket, the user is presented with a small form of questions. You can view the answers to these questions at the top of the ticket to help them right away.

Your goal is to try your best to help them resolve their issue using your technical knowledge and the resources available to you.

Available Resources:

We have plenty of resources in the #Specialist-Resources channel of the Discord server, as well at the bottom of this handbook.

Example Tech Support Ticket

U
User123
Today at 4:15 PM

Ticket Information:

Issue Type: Software Problem

Description: My computer keeps freezing randomly

OS: Windows 11

Started When: After last Windows update

S
Specialist
Today at 4:20 PM

Hello User123, I'll be assisting you with your freezing issue. Let's try some basic troubleshooting steps first.

Section V: Ticket Commands

Available Ticket Commands

These commands can be used in any ticket channel to manage and interact with the ticket system. All commands start with /ticket followed by the specific command.

/ticket add

Adds a new person to the ticket. Useful when you need assistance from another specialist or want to include a manager.

/ticket add @username

/ticket remove

Removes someone from the ticket. Use this when a specialist is no longer needed in the conversation.

/ticket remove @username

/ticket claim

Claims the ticket for yourself. This marks you as the responsible specialist handling this ticket.

/ticket claim

/ticket priority

Changes the priority of the ticket. Priorities help organize workload and identify urgent cases.

/ticket priority [low/medium/high]

Priority Indicators:
Low Priority - Standard issue
Medium Priority - Needs attention soon
High Priority - Urgent issue

/ticket rename

Renames the ticket to something more descriptive. Helps with organization and quick identification.

/ticket rename "New Ticket Name"

/ticket requestclose

Requests the ticket opener if they want to close the ticket. Use when you believe the issue is resolved.

/ticket requestclose

/ticket close

Closes the ticket immediately. Only use when absolutely certain the issue is resolved.

/ticket close

/ticket info

Displays information about the ticket including creation date, participants, and current status.

/ticket info

/ticket notes

Adds private notes to the ticket that only staff can see. Useful for internal documentation.

/ticket notes "Your private note here"

Section VI: Specialist Ranks

Rank Progression System

Our specialist team operates on a tiered ranking system that recognizes experience, dedication, and performance. Each rank comes with additional responsibilities and permissions.

Junior Specialist

Requirements:

Entry-level position obtained after successfully applying and being accepted into our staff team.

Purpose:

This probationary period allows you to demonstrate your technical knowledge, communication skills, and commitment to helping our community.

Permissions:
  • Basic ticket management
  • Access to support resources
  • Standard response capabilities

Specialist

Promotion Criteria:

Awarded to Junior Specialists who have consistently provided high-quality support, maintained excellent response times, and received positive feedback from users.

Responsibilities:

Full ticket handling capabilities, expected to handle more complex issues, and serve as a role model for Junior Specialists.

Additional Permissions:
  • Priority ticket assignment
  • Extended ticket management commands
  • Ability to mentor Junior Specialists

Senior Specialist

Achievement:

Earned by Specialists who have shown exceptional dedication, often after several months of outstanding service and leadership within the team.

Leadership Role:

Senior Specialists help guide the support team, assist with training, and handle the most complex technical issues.

Enhanced Permissions:
  • View all claimed tickets
  • Manual ticket deletion
  • Priority override capabilities
  • Advanced moderation tools

Head Specialist

Exclusive Position:

Only one Head Specialist serves at any time, selected from our most experienced and trusted Senior Specialists.

Primary Duties:
  • Oversee all staff promotions and demotions
  • Review and process staff applications
  • Develop and conduct training programs
  • Ensure quality control across all support interactions
Administrative Privileges:
  • Full ticket system control
  • Staff management capabilities
  • Policy implementation authority
  • Direct reporting to server administration

Ticket Management Permissions

Senior Specialists Can:
  • Delete tickets manually when necessary
  • View all currently claimed tickets
  • Reassign tickets between specialists
Head Specialist Can:
  • Perform all Senior Specialist ticket functions
  • Audit ticket history and specialist performance
  • Implement system-wide ticket policy changes
